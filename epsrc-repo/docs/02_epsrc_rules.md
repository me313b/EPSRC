# 02 — EPSRC Funding Service Rules (verified)

## Vision & Approach (the 6-page PDF)
One question: *"What are you hoping to achieve with and how will you deliver your proposed work?"* Vision = the what; Approach = the how.

**Format:** max **6 sides A4 + 1 page** diagrammatic workplan (Gantt); single spacing; margins ≥ 2 cm; **Arial 11 pt** (or equivalent sans-serif); single PDF ≤ 8 MB; filename = application number + "Vision and Approach"; enter "attachment supplied" in the text box.

## Vision assessment — three tests it must pass
1. **Jet Zero named** (UK policy hook)
2. **A quantified UK figure with a source** (ATI £3.2 bn AAM / 111,000 jobs / 10→15 kW/kg targets)
3. **Explicit pre-competitive / public-good justification** — why EPSRC, not industry/ATI, funds this (TRL 1–3; knowledge benefits the whole supply chain; no single firm captures enough value)

## Approach assessment — must show
- Every objective mapped to method + risk + named output (Gap n → On → WPn spine)
- **WP1 and WP2 in parallel from M1**, WP3 analytic from M1, only WP4 dependent (reviewers penalise sequential WPs)
- Modelled conventional baseline defined precisely (3-phase winding, discrete 2-level inverter, 270 V, same power/speed)
- Comprehensive risk table with mitigations
- Research environment + facility access evidenced
- Milestones + timeline → the +1 Gantt page

## R4RI Capability — exact four module headings
1. Contributions to the generation of new ideas, tools, methodologies, or knowledge
2. The development of others and maintenance of effective working relationships
3. Contributions to the wider research and innovation community
4. Contributions to broader research or innovation users and audiences and towards wider societal benefit

## Ethics section must address
No human/animal participants (brief) · dual use & defence (mandatory for aerospace — all outputs open, no defence contracts, Trusted Research compliance) · environmental (WEEE, net-zero contribution) · open access data · TRL 1–3 framing (outputs are knowledge, not certified hardware).

## Voice rules (apply to every section)
- Never "novel"/"innovative" as bare adjectives — say specifically what is new
- Never "this project will investigate" → "this programme will establish"
- No passive voice in opening paragraphs; lead with the problem before the solution
- Expand acronyms on first use, per section
- Unconfirmed items: `[PI TO CONFIRM: …]`
- State word count at the end of every drafted section
- UK English
